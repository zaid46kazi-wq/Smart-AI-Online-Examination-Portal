Q1

Which enzyme is responsible for unwinding the DNA double helix during replication?

Helicase (Your Choice)
DNA Polymerase

Explanation

Helicases are enzymes that bind and may even remodel nucleic acid or nucleic acid protein complexes. There are RNA and DNA helicases. DNA helicases are essential during DNA replication because they separate double-stranded DNA into single strands allowing each strand to be copied.

Q2

During which phase of the cell cycle does DNA replication primarily occur?

G2 Phase (Your Choice)
S Phase (Correct Answer)

Explanation

The S phase (Synthesis Phase) is the phase of the cell cycle in which DNA is replicated, occurring between G1 phase and G2 phase. Since accurate duplication of the genome is critical to successful cell division, the processes that occur during S-phase are tightly regulated.

Q3

What is the main byproduct of anaerobic respiration in muscle cells?

Lactic Acid (Your Choice)
Ethanol

Explanation

In mammals, anaerobic respiration in muscles produces lactic acid as a byproduct when oxygen levels are insufficient to sustain aerobic respiration. This process allows for continued ATP production under stress.
